Ko-Ling Immersive AR Hangul learning app for non-Koreans
Hangeul(Korean language) was created in 1443, but did you know that the education for Korean language is only about 60-year-history? As Korean culture spreads widely in the global MZ generation (Millennial + Z generation; people born in 1981~2010), the demand for effective educational content is increasing. Currently, many kinds of Hangul education are using method of just memorization. But wouldn’t foreigners in the MZ generation who value vivid experiences need a more interesting method?
Design Process
UX Reaserch So how about foreigners directly experience the context of Korean language that King Sejong created? If a user’s daily life is connected with learning through augmented reality story games, one might be able to understand Korean language more easily and excitingly. Based on these thoughts, we conducted the following UX research to explore the initial impression of the Korean language, learning methods, and challenges in learning.
Video Interview We interviewed 7 Korean learners to ask about studying foreign languages.
Interview An interview was conducted at Gyeongbokgung Palace. We aksed tourists about their methods how to study Korean.
Survey We conducted a survey for people who are interested in Korean on Reddit, Instagram and other web communities to know their recognization about Hangul.
Pain Point & Solution
Feelings of pressure Korean language learners usually sit down and look at their phone to learn Hangul.
Tedious memorization Korean learners often memorize shapes and sounds of characters without grasping their creation principles.
